Introduction

Bhutan is a traditional, cultural, and spiritually-minded kingdom hidden in the Himalayan foothills. At the head of this peaceful land are the revered monarchs of Bhutan, whose reigns have shaped the history of the nation and woven the fabric of its identity. From the time of the establishment of the monarchy in 1907 until the present, the kings of Bhutan have served as more than just rulers. They have been the architects of the country's modernization, its ideals, and its defenders of its unique past. They have never wavered in their commitment to maintaining the welfare and happiness of their people, as stated in the Gross National Happiness theory, despite times of great change during their reigns.In order to investigate the lives, legacies, and contributions of the Bhutanese kings, we will travel through time in this project. Each monarch has left an enduring impression on the kingdom and its people, from the visionary King Ugyen Wangchuck, who established the monarchy, to the adored King Jigme Khesar Namgyel Wangchuck, who guides Bhutan into the twenty-first century with compassion and wisdom. We explore the complex web of Bhutanese monarchy by combining historical narratives, cultural analyses, and first-hand experiences. We uncover inspiring stories of bravery, diplomacy, and devotion that have influenced a country's course. Come celebrate the blessings of the Bhutanese kings, whose reigns illuminate the past, inspire the present, and shape the future of this enchanting Himalayan kingdom.

    • Modernization and Democratic Transition: Under the reign of succeeding monarchs, Bhutan progressively modernised while preserving its traditions and cultural heritage. Significant changes were implemented in the mid-1900s, including the establishment of formal education, the abolition of serfdom, and the development of infrastructure. The 2008 adoption of a democratic constitution marked a significant turning point in Bhutan's history, resulting in the country's shift from a monarchy to a constitutional monarchy.
      Gross National Happiness (GNH) and Sustainable Development: Bhutan has received international recognition for its development philosophy, which prioritises the happiness and well-being of its people over purely economic objectives. The country has also been commended for its commitment to protecting the environment, maintaining a high level of forest cover, and putting sustainable development principles—such as hydropower projects—into practice.Interactions with the Outside World and Isolation: Bhutan pursued an isolationist policy for a large portion of its history, limiting those contacts. But Bhutan has been cautiously interacting with the world community in recent decades, all the while preserving its culture and independence.
